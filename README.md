## Seed Strategy (REVISED FOR DROUGHT)

### Recommended Blend:
**Fine Fescue (50-70%) + Kentucky Bluegrass (30-50%)**

**Why this ratio?**
- **Fine Fescue:** Most drought-tolerant cool-season grass. Critical for Aurora's water restrictions.
- **Kentucky Bluegrass:** Self-repairs, good play surface, but higher water needs (hence minority percentage).
- **Avoid Perennial Ryegrass:** Higher water requirements.

**Target amount:** 15-20 lbs total

## Seasonal Plan

## Spring (March - May): Maintenance Mode

**Goal:** Stabilize lawn, avoid mistakes.

**Actions:**
- Water 2x/week (Aurora restriction limit)
- Mow at 3-4 inches
- Let herbicides work (no seeding)
- No additional fertilizer
- Spot-treat weeds in late May IF lawn isn't heat-stressed

**Expectations:**
- Lawn WILL look weak and patchy. This is normal.
- You're in survival mode, not cosmetic mode.

## Summer (June - July): Survival Mode

**Goal:** Keep lawn alive with minimal input.

**Actions:**
- Mow at 3-4 inches
- Water **2 days/week** (Aurora max) - deep watering sessions
- NO fertilizer
- NO seeding (summer kills cool-season grass seed)
- Spot-treat weeds only if necessary

**Expectations:**
- Lawn WILL look brown and stressed. This is expected under drought conditions.
- Focus: Keep existing grass alive for fall renovation.

## Fall Execution Plan

### Front Yard (1,100 sq ft)

1. **Aerate heavily:**
   - Rent gas-powered core aerator
   - Multiple passes - aim for 2" deep plugs on 2" centers
   - Lawn should "look bad" after aeration (covered in plugs)

2. **Prep:**
   - Mow low to 2 inches
   - Rake/dethatch dead material aggressively
   - Remove debris
   - Apply 1/4 to 1/2 inch compost layer

3. **Seed:**
   - Use 4-5 lbs fine fescue/bluegrass blend
   - Scotts spreader setting: 5-6
   - Apply in two passes (cross pattern for even coverage)
   - Lightly rake seed into soil

4. **Water (germination phase):**
   - Daily light watering for 2-3 weeks (morning + afternoon if hot)
   - Keep surface moist, not muddy
   - Week 4+: Transition to deep watering 2x/week

---

### Backyard (Patch 3,000-4,000 sq ft ONLY)

1. **Select zones:**
   - Choose worst bare/thin areas
   - Mark boundaries with spray paint or flags

2. **Aerate selected zones:**
   - Same core aerator process as front yard
   - Only treat marked zones, not entire backyard

3. **Prep:**
   - Rake thin/bare areas
   - Lightly loosen soil surface
   - Apply 1/4 to 1/2 inch compost to treated zones

4. **Seed:**
   - Use 10-15 lbs fine fescue/bluegrass blend across all patches
   - Scotts spreader where practical, hand-spread tight areas
   - Lightly rake seed into soil

5. **Water (germination phase):**
   - Same protocol as front yard (daily light watering 2-3 weeks)

---

## Watering Plan After Seeding (CRITICAL)

### Weeks 1-3 (Germination):
- Water **1-2 times per day** (morning + afternoon if hot/dry)
- Light sessions (10-15 min) to keep surface moist
- **This requires Aurora to allow daily watering. Check restrictions first.**

### Week 4+ (Establishment):
- Transition to **deep watering 2x/week**
- Longer sessions (30-45 min per zone)
- Encourage deeper root growth

### First Mow:
- Wait until new grass reaches 3-4 inches
- Mow to 3 inches (do not scalp)
- Bag clippings for first 2 mows to prevent smothering seedlings
