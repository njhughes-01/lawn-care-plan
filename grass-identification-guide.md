# How to Identify Your Lawn Grass Type

## Quick Visual Check (Look at a Single Blade)

### Kentucky Bluegrass
- **Blade tip:** Boat-shaped (like a canoe prow)
- **Blade texture:** Soft, fine
- **Color:** Medium to dark green
- **Growth:** Spreads by underground rhizomes (self-repairs bare spots)
- **Common in Colorado:** YES - most common cool-season lawn grass

### Tall Fescue
- **Blade tip:** Pointed
- **Blade texture:** Coarse, wide (widest cool-season grass)
- **Blade underside:** Shiny/glossy with prominent veins
- **Color:** Medium green
- **Growth:** Bunch-type (clumps, doesn't spread)
- **Common in Colorado:** YES - especially high-traffic areas

### Fine Fescue (Creeping Red, Chewings, Hard)
- **Blade tip:** Pointed, very narrow
- **Blade texture:** Very fine, needle-like (thinnest cool-season grass)
- **Color:** Medium to dark green (blue-green tint)
- **Growth:** Some spread by rhizomes (creeping red), others bunch
- **Common in Colorado:** YES - often mixed with bluegrass for shade

### Perennial Ryegrass
- **Blade tip:** Pointed
- **Blade texture:** Glossy/shiny topside
- **Blade underside:** Prominent veins
- **Color:** Bright/shiny green
- **Growth:** Bunch-type (clumps)
- **Common in Colorado:** SOMETIMES - quick germination, used for overseeding

### Buffalograss (if you already have some)
- **Blade tip:** Pointed, curly
- **Blade texture:** Thin but curly/wavy
- **Color:** Gray-green
- **Growth:** Spreads by stolons (above-ground runners)
- **Dormant color:** Straw-colored/tan (brown most of year)
- **Common in Colorado:** YES - but usually intentionally planted

## Best Way to Tell: Take Photos

### What to do:
1. **Take close-up photo of a single grass blade** (hold blade flat, show tip shape)
2. **Take photo of lawn texture** (overall appearance, clumpy vs smooth)
3. **Take photo of any stolons/runners** (above-ground stems connecting plants)
4. **Send photos to me** - I can identify with image analysis

### When to check:
- **Best time:** When grass is actively growing (May-June or late August)
- **Right now (late March):** Grass may still be dormant/brown - harder to ID
