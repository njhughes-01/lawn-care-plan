## 📋 Revised Recommendations

### Immediate (Spring 2026)
1. **Do nothing aggressive.** Herbicides need to work.
2. Water only enough to prevent complete die-off (2x/week under restrictions).
3. Spot-treat weeds in June IF lawn isn't heat-stressed.

### Summer 2026
1. Survival mode. Accept that lawn will look rough.
2. **2 days/week watering MAX** (Aurora restrictions).
3. Deep watering sessions (longer duration, less frequent).
4. NO fertilizer.

### Fall 2026 Decision Point
**Before committing to renovation, check:**
- Are Aurora water restrictions still in effect?
- Can you water daily for 2-3 weeks post-seeding? (Germination requires consistent moisture)
- If restrictions prohibit new turf establishment, **DELAY until Spring 2027**.

### If Fall 2026 Renovation Proceeds:
**Seed Selection (REVISED):**
- **Primary:** Fine Fescue blend (50-70%) + Kentucky Bluegrass (30-50%)
  - Fine fescue is THE most drought-tolerant cool-season grass
  - Bluegrass for self-repair, but not dominant given water constraints
- **Avoid:** Perennial ryegrass (higher water needs)
- **Buy from:** Arkansas Valley Seed, Pawnee Buttes, or Southwest Seed (CSU-recommended local dealers)

**Core Aeration:**
- Rent gas-powered core aerator ($70-100/day from Home Depot/Lowes)
- Make 2" deep plugs on 2" centers
- Multiple passes - "lawn should look bad" after aeration
- Mark sprinkler heads first to avoid damage

**Front Yard (1,100 sq ft):**
- Mow low (2"), rake/dethatch aggressively
- Topdress: 1/4 to 1/2" compost (15-20 bags)
- Seed: 4-5 lbs fine fescue/bluegrass blend
- Spreader setting: 5-6 on Scotts spreader
- Water: Daily light watering for germination (2-3 weeks), then transition to deeper 2x/week

**Backyard (worst 3,000-4,000 sq ft patches only):**
- Select bare/thin zones only
- Light rake, loosen soil, 1/4-1/2" compost
- Seed: 10-15 lbs total
- Same watering protocol
